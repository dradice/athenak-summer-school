bio: J.J. Zanazzi is a 51 Pegasi b Postdoctoral Fellow at the Center for Exoplanets and Habitable Worlds at Penn State, joining the Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics as Assistant Professor in August 2026. He is a theoretical astrophysicist who studies the ways in which planets form, with an emphasis on orbital and fluid dynamics and how they shape the diverse architectures observed in exoplanetary systems. His broader interests include stellar binaries, astroseismology, and high-energy astrophysics. He received his PhD from Cornell University and held postdoctoral positions at UC Berkeley and the Canadian Institute for Theoretical Astrophysics before joining Penn State.
