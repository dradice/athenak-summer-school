bio: Maitraya Bhattacharyya is a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy whose work sits at the intersection of numerical relativity, neutrino physics, and gravitational-wave astrophysics. He develops and employs GPU-accelerated numerical codes to simulate binary neutron star mergers, with a focus on how neutrino flavor transformations alter the composition of the merger remnant, the neutron-richness of the ejected material, and the resulting heavy-element nucleosynthesis yields. His simulations have provided the first evidence that neutrino flavor conversions leave a detectable imprint on post-merger gravitational-wave signals, with implications for multimessenger observations of neutron star collisions.
