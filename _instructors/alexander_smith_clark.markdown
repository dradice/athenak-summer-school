bio: Alexander is a computational astrophysicist specializing in nuclear reaction networks and reactive hydrodynamics. He completed his PhD at Stony Brook University under the supervision of Michael Zingale, where his thesis focused on modeling astrophysical reactions with applications to multidimensional classical CO novae simulations. He is the lead developer of pynucastro, an open-source Python library for interactively creating and exploring astrophysical nuclear reaction networks. His research interests include nuclear astrophysics, reactive hydrodynamics, and general relativity.
